Lancashire councils collectively spend billions of pounds of public money every year. Yet until recently, there was no easy way for residents to see exactly where that money was going. Individual councils publish spending data in sprawling CSV files that are almost impossible for ordinary people to make sense of. That's why I built a spending transparency platform.

## What Is It?

It is a free, open spending transparency platform that tracks over 2.3 million transactions totalling more than twelve billion pounds across all 15 Lancashire councils, from the twelve district councils to Lancashire County Council, Blackpool, and Blackburn with Darwen. It takes raw council spending data and makes it searchable, sortable, and analysable by anyone.

But it goes further than just listing transactions. The platform uses forensic analysis techniques to flag potential issues that would otherwise go unnoticed.

## What It Reveals

The analysis across Lancashire's councils has uncovered some striking patterns. Duplicate payments, where the same invoice appears to have been paid more than once, are surprisingly common. Split payment evasion, where large purchases are broken into smaller amounts to avoid procurement thresholds, shows up across multiple councils. Supplier concentration is another concern: in some councils, a handful of suppliers dominate entire spending categories with little evidence of competitive tendering.

The platform also applies Benford's Law analysis to detect statistical anomalies in payment amounts, identifies year-end spending spikes that may indicate budget gaming, and cross-references suppliers against Companies House records to check for compliance and potential conflicts of interest.

## Why This Matters

Every pound of wasted public money is a pound that could have gone towards fixing a pothole, funding a youth service, or keeping council tax down. Transparency isn't about catching people out for the sake of it. It's about creating the accountability that leads to better decisions and better value for residents.

## How You Can Use It

You can search for specific suppliers, browse spending by department, explore the analysis findings, or dig into budget breakdowns. Everything is free and requires no registration.

If you spot something that doesn't look right, I want to hear about it. Informed residents asking informed questions is exactly how local government improves.
