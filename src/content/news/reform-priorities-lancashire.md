When I stood for election, I made a simple promise: to put Padiham and Burnley West first and to be honest with you about what's happening at County Hall. Here are the issues I'm focused on and what I'm doing about them.

## Value for Money

Council tax keeps going up, but do you feel like services are getting better? Most people I speak to don't. Lancashire County Council's budget runs into the billions, and I believe there is significant scope to deliver better outcomes without asking residents to pay more. That means challenging wasteful spending, questioning contracts that don't deliver, and making sure every department is held to account for results — not just activity.

## Better Roads and Local Services

The state of our roads is one of the most common complaints I hear. Potholes, delayed repairs, and poorly planned roadworks all have a real impact on daily life. I'm pushing for better prioritisation of road maintenance in our division and greater transparency about how highways budgets are allocated across the county.

Beyond roads, I want to see local services that actually work for the people who use them. Whether that's waste collection, planning decisions, or social care — residents deserve services that are responsive and efficient.

## Transparency and Accountability

I didn't just talk about transparency during my campaign — I built a platform to deliver it. Through [AI DOGE](https://aidoge.co.uk), anyone can now scrutinise how Lancashire's councils spend public money. As a councillor, I'm applying that same approach from the inside: asking questions in committee, requesting data, and publishing what I find.

## Supporting Our Communities

Padiham and Burnley West are communities with real character and real potential. I want to see local businesses supported rather than burdened by bureaucracy, town centres that are well-maintained and welcoming, and decisions made locally by people who understand local needs.

## Working for You

These aren't abstract policy positions — they're the things I hear about on doorsteps and in conversations with residents. If there's an issue that matters to you, please get in touch. I'm your representative, and I'm here to fight your corner.
