In May 2025, Reform UK won 53 of 84 seats at Lancashire County Council — the largest Reform group on any county council in England. Ten months in, here's what the data shows.

## What We Inherited

The previous administration left LCC with a **£986 million** annual budget, **declining reserves**, and spending patterns that had never been properly scrutinised.

| Metric | Figure |
|---|---|
| Net revenue budget | £985.9M |
| Total service expenditure | £2.48B |
| Reserves (March 2025) | £345M — down from £420M in 2022 |
| Council tax Band D | £1,736 — up 5% year-on-year |
| Dependency on grants | 63.3% |

Reserves have fallen every single year since 2021 — from £420M to £345M. That's **£75 million** burned through in four years under the previous administration. At current trajectory, they'd hit £318M by March 2026.

## What AI DOGE Found

Using the [AI DOGE platform](https://aidoge.co.uk/lancashire/lancashirecc/) I built before entering office, we've forensically analysed **753,220 transactions** totalling **£3.56 billion** across 20 months of LCC spending data.

The findings are stark:

- **£93.6M** in suspected duplicate payments across 15,452 transaction groups
- **£433.6M** in suspected split payment patterns across 439 instances
- **£134.9M** in round-number payments over £5,000 — a classic red flag
- **14.7x** year-end spending spike in March vs monthly average
- **33 contracts** awarded with weak competition, including a £110.8M electricity contract
- **HIGH supplier concentration** in Public Health (HHI score: 3,615)

These patterns largely predate Reform taking control. But they're now our responsibility to address — and we are.

| Service Area | Spend | Share |
|---|---|---|
| Education | £1,267M | 51% |
| Adult Social Care | £585M | 24% |
| Children's Social Care | £292M | 12% |
| Public Health | £98M | 4% |
| Environmental Services | £94M | 4% |
| Highways & Transport | £69M | 3% |
| Central Services | £42M | 2% |
| Cultural Services | £24M | 1% |

The top five suppliers alone account for over **£382 million**: NHS Lancashire & South Cumbria ICB (£171M), Lancashire Renewables (£71M), Suez Recycling (£59M), Lancashire Schools PFI (£44M), and Guardian Homecare (£38M).

## Reform vs Conservative: A Clear Contrast

The Conservatives held LCC for decades before losing control. Under their watch:

- **Reserves declined** year after year with no plan to arrest the fall
- **Supplier concentration** went unchecked — cosy relationships, limited competition
- **No spending transparency platform** existed. Residents had no way to see where their money went
- **Council tax rose consistently** while services were cut

Reform brought:

- **AI-powered forensic scrutiny** of every transaction, publicly accessible
- **A Lead Member for Finance & Resources** (myself) who built the transparency tools before entering office
- **53 seats** — a mandate for change, not tinkering
- **Data-driven decision-making** replacing backroom deal-making

## What Comes Next

The government's Local Government Reorganisation consultation closes on 26 March 2026. If approved, Lancashire's 15 councils could merge into new unitary authorities by April 2028. Under the two-unitary model, estimated net savings are **£79.4 million per year** — money that could go back into frontline services instead of duplicated bureaucracy.

In the meantime, we're getting on with the job. Every pound of public money should be accounted for. Every contract should face proper competition. Every resident should be able to see exactly where their council tax goes.

That's what Reform promised. That's what we're delivering.
